## TOKYO - ROOFTOP & URBAN GARDENS

### SPECIFIC LOCATIONS & LANDMARKS

#### **GINZA DISTRICT**

**Tokyu Plaza Ginza - Kiriko Terrace (11th Floor)**
- Elevation: 56 meters above ground level
- Features: Panoramic views of Sukiyabashi Crossing, Hibiya Station, and Ginza's Printemps
- Accessibility: Free admission, comfortable seating, seasonal illuminations
- Equipment: Tripod policies vary (confirm current regulations before shooting)
- Best for: Quiet, intimate fashion shoots with urban skyline backdrop

**GINZA SIX Rooftop Garden (13th Floor)**
- Size: Approximately 3,960 square meters corridor-style garden
- Accessibility: Free entry for all visitors
- Features: 360-degree views of Tokyo skyline, tripods not prohibited
- Best for: Wide-angle fashion editorials with expansive city views

**Mitsukoshi Ginza Rooftop (9th Floor)**
- Features: Open-air terrace with grass, trees, and plantings
- Atmosphere: Park-like feel with wooden terrace seating
- Best for: Natural, garden-inspired fashion shoots in urban setting

**Ginza Komatsu West Building Rooftop**
- Hours: 11:00-20:00 (may close in bad weather)
- Views: Yurakucho area and Imperial Palace grounds
- Best for: Heritage architecture backdrops

#### **ROPPONGI DISTRICT**

**Roppongi Hills - Tokyo City View Observation Deck**
- Signature view: Tokyo Tower
- Features: Rooftop helipad area (subject to closures)
- Best for: Iconic Tokyo landmark integration

**Tokyo Midtown Roppongi (6th Floor)**
- Features: Tranquil green space within mall-office complex
- Best for: Contrast of nature and contemporary architecture

### URBAN EDITORIAL USAGE & EXAMPLES

- **Professional Fashion Shoots**: Tokyo offers photoshoot spaces from polished studios to edgy rooftops with skyline views
- **Popular Districts for Fashion**: Warehouse-style studios in Kachidoki and Meguro are go-to spots for fashion editorials
- **Rooftop Photography Culture**: Australian photographer Benjamin Hung scaled rooftops throughout Shibuya and Shinjuku, capturing neon-drenched metropolis
- **Editorial Tours**: Tokyo offers urbex & rooftop photo tours where photographers explore upper reaches of the city

### LIGHTING & ARCHITECTURAL CHARACTERISTICS

**Daytime Lighting:**
- Golden/Blue hour: Optimal for warm, dramatic skyline shots
- Afternoon: Great for shadow play on crosswalks and urban geometry
- Rainy days: Excellent for moody, reflective street-level shots from rooftop perspective

**Evening/Night Lighting:**
- Ginza transforms after sunset with neon reflections and polished glass facades
- Evening illumination creates rich visual palette for nightscapes and candid portraits
- Ambient rooftop lighting provides soft fill light for portraits

**Architectural Features:**
- Modern minimalist rooftop gardens with wooden terraces
- Glass facades and reflective surfaces
- Mix of traditional and contemporary urban planning
- Geometric shapes and clean lines characteristic of Japanese modernism

**Recommended Equipment:**
- Wide-angle and telephoto lenses
- Polarizer to reduce glass reflections
- Handheld capability important (tripod restrictions vary by location)

### ACCESSIBILITY & PRACTICAL CONSIDERATIONS

**Access:**
- Most rooftop gardens are free to public
- Located in major shopping complexes (Tokyu Plaza, GINZA SIX, Mitsukoshi)
- Easily accessible via Tokyo Metro

**Restrictions:**
- Tripod policies vary by location - always confirm before shooting
- Commercial shoots require advance Japanese-language applications
- Cannot obstruct pedestrian traffic in busy districts

**Language Barrier:**
- Working with local fixers recommended for commercial permits
- Locations unlikely to accept filming requests without Japanese communication

**Weather:**
- Some rooftop gardens close in bad weather
- Best to check conditions before visiting

### PERMITS & COMMERCIAL PHOTOGRAPHY REGULATIONS

**Permit Requirements:**
- Commercial photography requires shooting permits from relevant authorities
- Applications must be in Japanese with detailed project plans and proposals
- Company seal or signature required on all applications

**Public Spaces:**
- Parks, private property, temples, shrines require permits
- Sidewalks: Can shoot without permit, but cannot set up tripods
- Application fees may apply for public land photography

**Processing:**
- Must apply in advance (timeline varies by location)
- Local production companies/fixers highly recommended
- Fixers have established relationships with locations and organizations

**Specific Restrictions:**
- Busy districts: Avoid obstructing pedestrian traffic
- Tripods often discouraged or banned in crowded areas
- Meiji Shrine: Prohibits commercial photography and on-location sessions

### BEST TIMES & SEASONS FOR SHOOTING

**Best Times of Day:**
- Golden hour (late afternoon): Warm, dramatic lighting on skyline
- Blue hour (evening): Neon lights begin illuminating streets
- Early morning: Fewer crowds, soft natural light
- Late evening: Full neon cityscape activation

**Best Seasons:**
- Spring (March-May): Cherry blossoms, pleasant temperatures, clear skies
- Autumn (September-November): Comfortable weather, crisp air, excellent visibility
- Winter (December-February): Clear skies, dramatic lighting, fewer tourists
- Summer (June-August): Humid, but vibrant atmosphere; rainy season June-July

**Avoid:**
- Rainy season (June-July) unless seeking moody, wet aesthetic
- Peak tourist hours on weekends

### WHAT MAKES THIS LOCATION UNIQUE FOR EDITORIAL

1. **Vertical Urban Landscape**: Tokyo's rooftop gardens offer elevated perspectives of one of world's most densely built cities
2. **Neon Aesthetic**: Unique blend of traditional Japanese design with hyper-modern neon-lit urbanity
3. **Free Access**: Unlike many cities, Tokyo's premium rooftop spaces are accessible to public
4. **Architectural Contrast**: Mix of minimalist Japanese design with maximalist urban density
5. **Night Photography**: Exceptional neon-lit nightscape opportunities unavailable in most cities
6. **Garden-Sky Integration**: Rooftop gardens blend nature with concrete jungle
7. **Fashion-Forward Culture**: Tokyo's cutting-edge fashion scene provides context for editorial work
8. **Scalable Intimacy**: Can shoot intimate portraits or wide environmental shots from same location

## STORM KING ART CENTER - OUTDOOR SCULPTURE PARK

### SPECIFIC LOCATIONS & LANDMARKS

**Location:** 1 Museum Road, New Windsor, New York (Hudson Valley)

**Size:** 500 acres of outdoor museum space

**Landscape Features:**
- Rolling meadows
- Wooded hillsides
- Paved and unpaved pathways
- Mown lawns
- Varied terrain with steep areas
- Open sky viewing

**Notable Sculptures:** Perhaps the largest collection of contemporary outdoor sculptures in the United States

### URBAN EDITORIAL USAGE & EXAMPLES

**Professional Fashion Shoots:**
- Confirmed Vogue magazine photoshoots
- The Knot magazine photoshoots
- Site fees start at $10,000 for editorial/commercial shoots
- Collaboration with major fashion and lifestyle publications

**Media Visibility:**
- Featured on Netflix's "Master of None"
- Widely recognized as premier outdoor sculpture destination
- Popular for high-end editorial and commercial photography

**Personal Photography:**
- $400 fee for professional photographers (engagement, family photos)
- Must provide COI insured up to $1,000,000
- Release form required stating non-commercial use
- Includes admission for up to four guests

### LIGHTING & ARCHITECTURAL CHARACTERISTICS

**Natural Lighting:**
- Open sky provides consistent, even daylight
- Rolling hills create natural shadows and dimension
- Wooded areas offer dappled light and shade
- 500 acres ensure diverse lighting conditions throughout property

**Sculptural Architecture:**
- Large-scale contemporary sculptures create dramatic backdrops
- Site-specific commissions integrate with landscape
- Mix of metal, stone, and organic materials
- Geometric and organic forms provide editorial variety

**Landscape Character:**
- Pastoral Hudson Valley setting
- Mountain and valley views
- Seasonal foliage changes (spring blossoms, autumn colors, winter starkness)
- Natural grasslands and cultivated gardens

### ACCESSIBILITY & PRACTICAL CONSIDERATIONS

**Accessibility Features:**
- Free wheelchair-accessible tram available
- Tram stops throughout site (get on/off at scheduled stops)
- ADA-accessible pathways from new parking lot
- 12 electric vehicle charging stations
- Dedicated bus and rideshare drop-off zones
- Recently completed $53 million accessibility renovation

**Terrain:**
- Varied: rolling meadows, hills, wooded areas
- Mix of paved paths, gravel paths, and mown lawn
- Visitor map indicates paved/unpaved paths and steep areas
- Good walking shoes essential

**Service Animals:**
- ADA Title III service dogs welcome
- Pets, emotional support animals, therapy animals not permitted

**Parking:**
- Included with general admission ticket
- Modern facilities with EV charging

### PERMITS & COMMERCIAL PHOTOGRAPHY REGULATIONS

**Personal Photography:**
- Welcome for personal, non-commercial use
- Flash photography restricted in galleries
- No permission required for visitor snapshots

**Professional/Commercial Shoots:**
- Must obtain prior written approval
- Site fees based on scope, typically starting at $10,000 for editorial/commercial
- Prohibited without permission: professional recording, commercial portfolios, recording for hire
- Contact Film & Photo Shoots department

**Personal Professional Shoots (Engagement, Family):**
- $400 administration fee
- Includes admission for up to four guests
- Certificate of Insurance required (up to $1,000,000)
- Signed release form required (non-commercial use)

**Restrictions:**
- Professional recording prohibited without approval
- Commercial use of any site element requires written permission

### BEST TIMES & SEASONS FOR SHOOTING

**Operating Season:**
- Open: May through November (seasonal museum)
- Closed: December through March
- 2025 season ends: November 30
- 2026 season begins: April 1

**Hours:**
- Monday, Wednesday-Sunday: 10AM-4PM
- Closed Tuesdays
- Last admission: 3PM
- Members early access: 9:30AM

**Best Days to Visit:**
- Weekdays (Wednesday-Friday) for fewer crowds
- Weekends always crowded
- Advance tickets recommended

**Seasonal Characteristics:**

**Spring (April-May):**
- Flowering trees and blossoms
- Vibrant greenery
- Ideal for first-time visitors
- Fresh, renewal aesthetic

**Summer (June-August):**
- Full foliage and lush landscapes
- Longest days for extended golden hour
- Peak visitor season (more crowded)

**Autumn (September-November):**
- Fall foliage colors
- Dramatic seasonal transformation
- Comfortable temperatures
- Rich, warm color palette

**Weather:**
- Open rain or shine
- Check local weather before visiting
- Dress appropriately for outdoor conditions

### WHAT MAKES THIS LOCATION UNIQUE FOR EDITORIAL

1. **Scale**: 500 acres offers unlimited compositional variety and privacy
2. **Art Integration**: Fashion meets fine art in monumental sculpture settings
3. **Hudson Valley Landscape**: Prestigious, pastoral setting with cultural cachet
4. **Seasonal Transformation**: Dramatically different aesthetic each season
5. **Exclusive Access**: Commercial shoots ensure privacy and control
6. **Cultural Prestige**: Association with world-class contemporary art
7. **Natural Architecture**: Sculptures create unique geometric and organic backdrops
8. **Editorial Heritage**: Established history with Vogue and major publications
9. **Accessibility**: Despite vast size, wheelchair-accessible tram system ensures reach
10. **Weather Independence**: Open rain or shine creates diverse atmospheric possibilities

- Character: Blend of rich history with modern inclusivity

- Best for: Authentic Copenhagen street life and diversity

**Superkilen Park:**
- Architect: BIG Architects (Bjarke Ingels Group)

**Tourist Promotional Photography:**
- Permitted in streets and open squares with no special authorization
- No permit needed for tourism content

**Commercial Fashion Shoots:**
- Permit required from location owner or public authority for all commercial shoots
- Applications must contain exact information: who, what, where, when

**B-Roll Shoots:**
- Not affecting public movement: No permit required
- Requiring exclusive use of public space: Permit required
- Large lights, track laying, traffic control: ~2 weeks permit processing

**Commercial Use of Public Spaces:**
- Cannot hire public spaces for purely commercial/private events
- Market rent may apply in parks for commercial/profitable events
- No exclusive commercial takeovers of public areas

**Indoor Filming:**
- Obtain permission from shopkeepers
- Private property requires direct permission

**Practical Tips:**
- Work with local production companies for efficient permitting
- Plan ahead for commercial shoots (minimum 2 weeks for complex setups)
- Small, handheld shoots more likely to be permitted

## MIAMI ART DECO DISTRICT

### SPECIFIC LOCATIONS & LANDMARKS

**Miami Beach Architectural District (South Beach):**
- Boundaries: Atlantic Ocean (east), Sixth Street (south), Alton Road (west), Collins Canal and Dade Boulevard (north)
- Significance: Largest collection of Art Deco buildings in the world
- Era: Buildings from 1930s and 1940s

#### **OCEAN DRIVE**

**Location:** 5th to 15th Streets, fronting Lummus Park
- Character: Famous setting for fashion photography
- Features: Iconic Art Deco hotels, pastel-colored buildings, geometric shapes, retro appeal
- Park: Lummus Park (public park and beach) provides green space
- Best for: Classic Art Deco fashion editorials

**The Colony Hotel (736 Ocean Drive):**
- Era: Built in the 1930s
- Distinction: Most photographed hotel in South Beach
- Character: Quintessential Art Deco facade
- Best for: Iconic, recognizable South Beach aesthetic

#### **BEACH & WATERFRONT**

**Lifeguard Towers:**
- Quantity: Approximately 40 unique Art Deco lifeguard stands
- Features: Colorful, individually designed structures
- Best for: Vibrant, playful fashion editorials with beach context

**Lummus Park Beach:**
- Features: White sand, public beach, palm trees
- Location: Directly adjacent to Ocean Drive
- Best for: Beach fashion, swimwear, lifestyle editorials

#### **ADDITIONAL NOTABLE LOCATIONS**

**Vizcaya Museum and Gardens:**
- Style: Mediterranean-style mansion
- Features: Expansive gardens, historic architecture
- Best for: High-fashion, romantic, heritage-inspired editorials

**Wynwood Walls:**
- Character: Enormous and colorful murals
- Best for: Street art backdrop, urban fashion, culture-infused photography
- Style: Contemporary contrast to Art Deco heritage

### URBAN EDITORIAL USAGE & EXAMPLES

**Versace Legacy:**
- Historic connection: Gianni Versace lived and tragically died in mansion on Ocean Drive
- Editorial impact: Versace helped propel South Beach's pastel-hued, tropical lifestyle
- Ocean Drive became backdrop for "every hot new campaign" in the 1990s

**"South Beach Stories" Campaign (1992-1993):**
- Photographer: Doug Ordway
- Client: Versace (Gianni and Donatella)
- Locations: Crandon Park (Key Biscayne), International Swimming Hall of Fame (Fort Lauderdale)
- Models: Christy Turlington among others
- Material: "Huge suitcases filled with leather and printed silks"
- Book: Published 1993 by Versace family
- Impact: Defined South Beach as fashion destination

**Versace Campaign Shoots:**
- Versace Uomo campaign: Crandon Park, Key Biscayne (March 1992)
- Christy Turlington shoot: International Swimming Hall of Fame, Fort Lauderdale (November 1992)

**"South Beach Stories" Exhibition (Art Basel Miami):**
- Character: Neon-hued installation celebrating Versace's Miami history
- Color palette: Jungle green, lime, orange, Caribbean blue (Miami-inspired)
- Content: Early '90s campaign imagery by Doug Ordway, original runway designs

**Contemporary Fashion Use:**
- Ideal fusion of modern and nostalgic design
- Popular for: Brand campaigns, editorial shoots, influencer content
- Getty Images: Thousands of Art Deco district fashion photography stock images

### PERMITS & COMMERCIAL PHOTOGRAPHY REGULATIONS

**Permit Requirements:**
- All commercial film, TV, video, online media, radio, drone filming and photography must obtain Film & Print permit from City of Miami Beach

**Citywide Permit (Ideal for Fashion Shoots):**
- Widely used by fashion shoots and portrait photographers
- Cast & crew: Under 12 people
- Equipment: Handheld only
- Cannot interfere with pedestrian/vehicular traffic
- Cannot impact businesses
- Allowed equipment: Camera, tripod, reflectors, battery-operated lights
- Not allowed: Generators, grip equipment, cabling

**Exemptions (No Permit Required):**
- Single camera/photographer
- No models, assistants, or crew
- Handheld equipment only
- No large props
- Not blocking sidewalks or businesses

**Application Timing:**
- Photography permits: Submit by noon the day prior to shoot
- Film permits: Minimum 4 business days prior (strongly suggested)

**Additional Considerations:**
- Miami Beach has stricter regulations than Miami proper
- Commercial shoots near water or Ocean Drive may have additional requirements
- Each location may have own regulations and permit requirements

## LISBON, PORTUGAL

### SPECIFIC LOCATIONS & LANDMARKS

#### **ALFAMA DISTRICT**

**Character:** Oldest district in Lisbon, most charismatic and photogenic
- Features: Winding narrow streets, vibrant colors, unique spirit
- Architecture: Houses decorated with gorgeous azulejo tiles
- Atmosphere: Dream location for editorial photography
- Accessibility: Less crowded than tourist areas, more authentic
- Best for: Historic Lisbon aesthetic, traditional Portuguese culture

**Miradouro de Santa Luzia:**
- Features: Viewpoint with panoramic views, azulejo tile decoration
- Popular for: Tram 28 photography
- Best for: Elevated shots of Alfama rooftops

**Rua das Escolas Gerais:**
- Character: Steep, winding street through Alfama
- Popular for: Tram 28 photography
- Best for: Classic Lisbon street scenes

#### **CHIADO DISTRICT**

**Character:** Historic neighborhood with elegant architecture
- Features: Literary cafes, theaters, bookstores

### PERMITS & COMMERCIAL PHOTOGRAPHY REGULATIONS

**Permit Requirements:**

**Who Needs Permits:**
- Associations, educational establishments, companies, citizens
- Filming or photographing in public or municipal areas for commercial use

**What Requires Permits:**
- Commercial photoshoots with models, equipment (tripods, reflectors, light stands)
- Large-scale photography for promotional/business use
- Shoots taking up public space

**Small-Scale Non-Commercial:**
- Public locations (streets, parks): Usually no permit needed
- Iconic landmarks, historic sites: May require permits and fees for commercial work

**Licensing Fees:**
- Administrative fee: €450.05 per application
- Public space occupation (up to 10m²): €63.80 per day
- Surplus area 10-100m²: €4.55 per m²/day
- Surplus area greater than 100m²: €2.25 per m²/day

**Processing Time:**
- License decisions: Up to 3 working days from day after application submission

**Application Process:**
- Submit detailed information about project
- Specify location, dates, space requirements
- Pay applicable fees

**General Rights:**
- Photography and filming generally allowed in public spaces
- Must respect individual privacy
- Cannot use content for defamatory purposes or unauthorized personal gain

### GENERAL FASHION EDITORIAL BEST PRACTICES

1. **Scout in advance:** Visit locations before shoot day to understand light, crowds, access
