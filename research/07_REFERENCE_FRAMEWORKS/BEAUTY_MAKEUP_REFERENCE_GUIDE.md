## 1. Makeup Style Categories

### Natural/Everyday Makeup
**Description:** Enhances natural beauty with minimal, subtle products
- Light foundation or tinted moisturizer
- Barely-there eyeshadow
- Light natural lip color
- Subtle or no fake lashes
- Focus on fresh, clean appearance
- Often called "Second Skin" or "No-Makeup Makeup"

**Best For:** Daily wear, professional settings, casual events

### Soft Glam
**Description:** Polished enhancement with subtle drama
- Flawless, glowing complexion
- Soft, blended eyeshadow
- Defined but not dramatic features
- No harsh lines or heavy finishes
- Touch of sophistication
- Step up from natural with more layers

**Best For:** Weddings (guest), date nights, cocktail parties

### Full Glam
**Description:** Bold, dramatic makeup with maximum impact
- Multiple layers of product
- Heavy contouring and baking
- Thick strip lashes or dramatic volume
- Cut crease or smokey eye techniques
- Bold lips
- Statement-making features

**Best For:** Evening events, red carpet, special occasions, photography

### Editorial Makeup
**Description:** Artistic, experimental looks for visual storytelling
- Bold, experimental color choices
- Abstract shades and vibrant colors
- Pushes boundaries of traditional beauty
- Theme or story-driven
- May incorporate non-traditional materials
- Focuses on creative expression

**Best For:** Magazine spreads, fashion shoots, artistic photography

### Avant-Garde Makeup
**Description:** Extreme artistic expression, unconventional techniques
- Non-traditional techniques and colors
- Unexpected textures and materials
- Dramatic shapes and structures
- Extensive use of accessories
- More art than enhancement
- Frequently incorporates elements like rhinestones, feathers, metallic foil

**Best For:** High fashion, runway shows, art projects, experimental shoots

### Bridal Makeup
**Description:** Timeless, photograph-friendly enhancement

**Classic Bridal:**
- Soft, neutral eyeshadows
- Subtle contouring
- Timeless shades (soft pinks, nudes)
- Dewy foundation
- Long-lasting formulation
- Camera-ready finish

**Romantic Bridal:**
- Rosy tones
- Glowing skin
- Fluttery lashes
- Peachy and mauve colors
- Soft, feminine, dreamy
- Subtle shimmer

**Best For:** Weddings, engagement photos, formal portraits

### Fantasy/Sci-Fi Makeup
**Description:** Otherworldly, imaginative character creation
- Non-realistic color palettes
- Prosthetics and special effects
- Creature and character makeup
- Imaginative themes
- Extreme creativity

**Best For:** Cosplay, themed shoots, film/TV, Halloween

## 2. Skin Finish Terminology

### Matte Finish
**Characteristics:**
- Smooth, shine-free appearance
- No light reflection
- Flat, non-reflective surface
- Minimizes pores
- Reduces oil appearance
- Provides maximum coverage

**Best For:** Oily skin, photography, long wear
**Products:** Matte foundations, powder finishes, oil-control primers

### Dewy Finish
**Characteristics:**
- Radiant, luminous glow
- Mimics well-moisturized skin
- Post-facial appearance
- Natural oil reflection
- Light-reflecting properties
- Fresh, hydrated look

**Best For:** Dry skin, natural looks, youthful appearance
**Products:** Luminous foundations, liquid highlighters, facial oils

### Satin Finish
**Characteristics:**
- Between matte and dewy
- Subtle glow without shine
- Not flat, not reflective
- Radiant but controlled
- Balanced finish
- Natural-looking

**Best For:** Normal to combination skin, versatile looks
**Products:** Satin foundations, cream blushes, semi-matte products

### Luminous Finish
**Characteristics:**
- Lit-from-within glow
- Light coverage foundation
- True complexion shows through
- Natural radiance
- Subtle light reflection
- Less reflective than dewy

**Best For:** Healthy skin, natural makeup looks, editorial work
**Products:** Illuminating primers, BB creams, light-coverage foundations

### Additional Skin Descriptors
- **Airbrushed:** Flawless, smooth, pore-less appearance
- **Porcelain:** Smooth, perfect, doll-like skin
- **Glossy/Wet Look:** High-shine, just-dipped-in-water appearance
- **Velvet:** Soft, luxurious, touchable texture
- **Powdered:** Set, matte, traditionally finished
- **Glass Skin:** Korean beauty trend - ultra-smooth, reflective like glass
- **Skin-Like:** Undetectable, natural texture preserved

## 3. Eye Makeup Looks & Techniques

### Classic Eye Looks

#### Smokey Eye
**Description:** Deep, blended shadows creating sultry, smoldering effect
- Dark, smoked-out eyeshadows
- Gradient from light to dark
- Heavily blended
- Often uses blacks, grays, deep browns
- Dramatic and captivating
- Timeless evening look

**Variations:**
- Classic Black Smokey
- Brown/Neutral Smokey
- Colored Smokey (purple, blue, green)
- Soft/Light Smokey

#### Cat Eye
**Description:** Elongated, feline-inspired eye shape
- Dramatic winged eyeliner
- Emphasizes almond eye shape
- Upward flick at outer corner
- Makes eyes appear elongated
- Can be subtle or dramatic
- All cat eyes have wings, not all wings are cat eyes

**Variations:**
- Classic Cat Eye
- Double Wing
- Graphic Cat Eye
- Floating Cat Eye

#### Cut Crease
**Description:** Sharp definition between lid and crease
- Contrasting shades on lid vs. crease
- Often uses concealer for sharp line
- Creates depth and dimension
- Dramatic, editorial effect

**Variations:**
- **Full Cut Crease:** Entire crease defined from inner to outer corner
- **Half Cut Crease:** Only inner half defined, softer and more wearable
- **Double Cut Crease:** Two distinct crease lines

#### Halo Eye
**Description:** Light center with dark inner and outer corners
- Highlighted center lid
- Darker shades frame the light
- Creates "halo" effect
- Combines cut crease with central highlight
- Adds dimension and makes eyes appear larger

#### Foxy Eye
**Description:** Lifted, elongated eye shape mimicking fox eyes
- Snatched, lifted appearance
- Upward-sweeping liner
- Creates illusion of facelift
- Almond-shaped enhancement
- Uses eyeliner, concealer, shadow strategically

### Eye Makeup Techniques

#### Tightlining
- Applying liner between lashes
- Makes lashes appear thicker at roots
- Fills lash line gaps
- Subtle enhancement

#### Baking
- Applying heavy powder to set concealer
- Letting powder "bake" for several minutes
- Brushing away excess
- Creates crease-free, flawless finish
- Popular under eyes and on T-zone

#### Gradient/Ombre Eyes
- Smooth color transition
- Light to dark or vice versa
- Blended seamlessly
- Creates depth

#### Graphic Liner
- Bold, artistic eyeliner shapes
- Non-traditional placement
- Geometric or abstract designs
- Editorial and fashion-forward

#### Floating Crease
- Color placed above natural crease
- Often seen on hooded eyes
- Creates visible color when eyes open
- Modern editorial technique

### Eye Intensity Descriptors
- **Bare/Nude Lid:** No visible eyeshadow
- **Subtle/Soft:** Light wash of color
- **Defined:** Clear color placement, some depth
- **Smokey:** Blended, smudged, sultry
- **Dramatic:** Bold, intense, statement-making
- **Editorial/Artistic:** Unconventional, creative

## 4. Lip Color & Texture Terminology

### Lip Finishes

#### Matte Finish
**Characteristics:**
- Flat, non-shiny appearance
- No light reflection
- Velvety texture
- Dense pigment load
- Full coverage
- Bold, uniform color
- Long-lasting wear
- Less likely to smudge

**Best For:** Bold looks, longevity, photography
**Considerations:** Can be drying, requires lip prep

#### Glossy Finish
**Characteristics:**
- Reflective, luminous shine
- High-shine appearance
- Light-reflecting
- Creates illusion of fuller lips
- Hydrated appearance
- More emollients in formula

**Best For:** Casual looks, youthful appearance, dimension
**Considerations:** Requires reapplication, can transfer

#### Satin Finish
**Characteristics:**
- Semi-matte with hint of shine
- Balance between matte and glossy
- Smooth application
- Rich color payoff
- Touch of luminosity
- Emollient base (oils and butters)
- Comfortable wear

**Best For:** Everyday wear, versatile looks, most occasions
**Considerations:** Good middle ground option

#### Velvet Finish
**Characteristics:**
- Matte appearance with soft texture
- Cushioned, whipped feel
- More comfortable than traditional matte
- Non-shiny but not flat
- Luxurious feel

**Best For:** Comfort with matte aesthetic
**Considerations:** Premium option, combines best of both worlds

### Additional Lip Textures
- **Cream:** Rich, moisturizing, semi-opaque
- **Sheer:** Light color payoff, natural tint
- **Lacquer:** High-shine, intense color
- **Stain:** Long-lasting pigment, no texture
- **Liquid:** Highly pigmented, various finishes
- **Metallic:** Reflective, shimmer particles
- **Pearl/Shimmer:** Iridescent, light-catching

### Lip Color Categories

#### Nude Lips
- Skin-tone matching or close
- Natural appearance
- Ranges from pale beige to deep brown
- "My lips but better" (MLBB)

#### Red Lips
- Classic, timeless statement
- Variations: Blue-red, orange-red, burgundy, scarlet, cherry, wine

#### Pink Lips
- Ranges from baby pink to hot pink
- Romantic to bold
- Variations: Bubblegum, rose, mauve, fuchsia

#### Berry/Plum Lips
- Deep purples and wine tones
- Sophisticated and bold
- Fall/winter favorite

#### Brown Lips
- 90s resurgence
- Ranges from nude-brown to chocolate
- Often paired with lip liner

#### Dark/Vampy Lips
- Deep burgundy, black, oxblood
- Dramatic, edgy
- Statement-making

#### Ombre/Gradient Lips
- Korean beauty trend
- Darker outer edges, lighter center
- Blurred, stained appearance
- Creates fuller-looking lips

#### Overlining
- Drawing outside natural lip line
- Creates illusion of fuller lips
- Popular in current trends

## 5. Blush & Contour Application Styles

### Blush Application Techniques

#### Draping
**Description:** Sculpting with blush instead of contour
- Applies blush from temples to cheekbones
- C-shaped application
- Deeper blush in hollows
- Lighter blush on apples and temples
- Originally called "color glow" by Way Bandy (1970s)
- Creates lifted, defined appearance
- Brings out natural face drapes

**Effect:** Sculpted cheekbones, lifted face, colorful definition

#### Apple of Cheeks
**Description:** Traditional blush placement
- Applied to roundest part when smiling
- Creates youthful, fresh appearance
- Circular or soft application

#### Blown-Out Blush
**Description:** Extended, diffused blush placement
- Blush swept across larger area
- Soft, blurred edges
- Current trend: extending to temples
- Creates flushed, romantic look

#### Sunburnt/Sun-Kissed Blush
**Description:** Placement mimicking natural sun flush
- Across nose bridge and cheeks
- Creates healthy, outdoorsy glow
- Often uses peachy or coral tones

### Contour Styles

#### Sculpting
**Description:** Traditional contouring for definition
- Darker product in hollows
- Creates shadows and depth
- Defines cheekbones, jawline, nose
- Can be subtle or dramatic

#### Soft/Natural Contour
- Barely-there definition
- Cream products
- Well-blended
- Mimics natural shadows

#### Dramatic/Instagram Contour
- Heavy definition
- Often includes baking
- Multiple shades
- High-contrast
- Photography-focused

#### Bronzer Contouring
- Uses bronzer instead of cool-toned contour
- Warmer, more natural
- Sun-kissed effect
- Less stark than traditional contour

### Blush Color Families
- **Pink:** Youthful, romantic, ranges from baby to hot pink
- **Peach/Coral:** Warm, fresh, universally flattering
- **Mauve/Rose:** Sophisticated, subtle
- **Berry/Plum:** Deep, dramatic, editorial
- **Orange/Tangerine:** Bold, modern, trendy
- **Brown/Terracotta:** Natural, earthy, warm

## 6. Highlighter & Glow Techniques

### Strobing
**Description:** Highlighting technique without contouring
- Uses highlighter to define high points
- Creates camera-flash effect
- Luminous, dewy complexion
- Focuses on light-catching areas
- Definition without dark contour

**Application Points:**
- Tops of cheekbones
- Bridge of nose
- Cupid's bow
- Brow bone
- Inner corners of eyes

### Wet Look/Glossy Skin
**Description:** High-shine, just-dipped appearance
- Extreme luminosity
- Glossy, reflective finish
- Pools of highlighter
- Otherworldly glow
- Often uses setting spray on brush

**Effect:** Editorial, oceanic glow, dreamy appearance

### Natural Glow/Lit From Within
**Description:** Subtle radiance
- Minimal highlighter
- Skin-like finish
- Healthy appearance
- No obvious shimmer
- Modern 2025 interpretation

### Highlighter Finishes
- **Shimmer:** Fine light-reflecting particles
- **Metallic:** Intense, reflective shine
- **Pearl:** Iridescent, color-shifting
- **Glitter:** Chunky, sparkly particles
- **Satin:** Soft sheen, no sparkle
- **Liquid:** Wet, glossy appearance
- **Powder:** Traditional, buildable

### Placement Styles
- **High Cheekbones Only:** Classic, subtle
- **Full Face:** Forehead, nose, chin, cheeks
- **Targeted:** Specific features (nose tip, cupid's bow)
- **All-Over Glow:** Mixed into foundation or applied everywhere

## 7. Makeup Intensity Spectrum

### No-Makeup Makeup (Minimal)
**Description:** Makeup that appears invisible
- Enhances without obvious products
- "Second skin" effect
- Minimal layers
- Focus on skincare
- Natural texture preserved

**Products Used:**
- Tinted moisturizer or very light foundation
- Cream blush (barely visible)
- Mascara (possibly)
- Lip balm or nude lip
- Groomed brows

**Goal:** Look like you're not wearing makeup

### Natural/Everyday (Light)
**Description:** Subtle enhancement, polished appearance
- Light coverage foundation
- Neutral eyeshadow
- Natural-looking lashes
- Nude or pink lips
- Defined brows

**Goal:** Enhanced but natural version of yourself

### Soft Glam (Medium)
**Description:** Noticeable but elegant makeup
- Full coverage foundation
- Defined eyes (liner, shadow)
- False lashes or dramatic mascara
- Contour and highlight
- Statement lip or eye (not both)

**Goal:** Polished, camera-ready, sophisticated

### Full Glam (Maximum)
**Description:** Heavy, dramatic makeup
- Full coverage, contoured foundation
- Baking technique
- Cut crease or heavy eye look
- Dramatic lashes
- Overdrawn lips
- Multiple layers throughout

**Goal:** Statement-making, transformative

### Editorial/Artistic (Experimental)
**Description:** Makeup as art
- Non-traditional colors
- Experimental placement
- May sacrifice wearability for artistry
- Theme-driven
- Conceptual

**Goal:** Creative expression, storytelling, visual impact

## 8. Era-Specific Beauty Looks

### 1940s - Old Hollywood Glamour
**Characteristics:**
- Flawless cream foundation (mask effect)
- Bold, sculpted eyebrows
- Winged eyeliner (short, subtle)
- Minimal eyeshadow (neutral lids)
- Scarlet red lips (focal point)
- Soft pink or orange blush
- Matte finish overall
- Elegant, polished

**Icons:** Elizabeth Taylor, Ava Gardner, Rita Hayworth
**Modern Interpretation:** Classic red lip, winged liner, flawless base

### 1950s - Classic Glamour
**Characteristics:**
- Perfect winged eyeliner
- Defined, arched brows
- Light neutral eyeshadow
- True red lipstick
- Peachy blush on cheekbones
- Porcelain skin
- Feminine, refined

**Icons:** Marilyn Monroe, Grace Kelly, Audrey Hepburn
**Modern Interpretation:** Vintage-inspired bridal, retro glam

### 1960s - Mod & Dramatic
**Characteristics:**
- Bold graphic eyeliner
- Heavy lashes (top and bottom)
- Pale lips
- Cut crease techniques
- Twiggy-inspired doll eyes
- Minimalist cheeks
- Emphasis on eyes

**Icons:** Twiggy, Edie Sedgwick
**Modern Interpretation:** Graphic liner, dramatic lashes, pale lips

### 1970s - Disco Glam & Boho
**Characteristics:**
- Glitter and shimmer everywhere
- Bold, colorful eyeshadow (blues, greens, purples)
- Winged liner
- Bushy, bold brows
- Bronzed, sun-dipped skin
- Warm nude lips with gloss
- Long, voluminous lashes (top and bottom)
- Vibrant blush (pink, coral)

**Icons:** Donna Summer, Diana Ross, Cher
**Modern Interpretation:** Disco glitter, bronze goddess, bohemian earth tones

### 1980s - Bold & Experimental
**Characteristics:**
- Bright, punchy colors
- Heavy blush draping
- Bold brows
- More-is-more philosophy
- Bright lips (hot pink, red)
- Dramatic eye makeup
- Experimental placement

**Icons:** Madonna, Cyndi Lauper
**Modern Interpretation:** Neon revival, maximalist makeup

### 1990s - Grunge & Minimalism
**Characteristics:**
- Matte brown lipsticks
- Dark lip liner with lighter lip
- Thin, plucked eyebrows
- Neutral eyeshadow
- Matte face (not dewy)
- Deep reds, browns, mauve lips
- Minimal eye makeup
- Heroin chic aesthetic

**Icons:** Drew Barrymore, Kate Moss, Christina Aguilera
**Modern Interpretation:** Brown lips, thin brows revival, grunge aesthetic

### Y2K/Early 2000s - Frosted & Glossy
**Characteristics:**
- Frosted eyeshadow (blues, silvers, pastels)
- Face glitter
- Ultra-glossy lips (candy pink, caramel nude)
- Thin eyebrows
- Heavy blush on apples
- Shimmery, iridescent finishes
- White shadow in inner corner (unblended)
- Rhinestones and embellishments

**Icons:** Britney Spears, Christina Aguilera, Paris Hilton
**Modern Interpretation:** Y2K revival, glossy lips, frosted lids

### 2010s - Instagram Glam
**Characteristics:**
- Heavy contouring
- Baking technique
- Bold brows (Instagram brow)
- Cut crease eyes
- Overlining lips
- Full glam aesthetic
- Matte lips
- Highlighting and strobing

**Modern Interpretation:** Still current, full glam standard

### 2020s - Diverse & Natural
**Characteristics:**
- Clean girl aesthetic
- Glossy, dewy skin
- Natural brows
- Minimalist or maximalist (both coexist)
- Latte makeup
- Glass skin
- Faux freckles
- TikTok-driven trends

**Current:** Soft glam, natural enhancement, skincare-focused

## 9. Cultural Beauty Aesthetics

### K-Beauty (Korean)
**Philosophy:** Gradual skin improvement, healthy barrier, dewy radiance

**Characteristics:**
- Multi-step skincare routine (10+ steps)
- Glass skin (ultra-smooth, reflective)
- Dewy, luminous finish
- Gradient/ombre lips
- Straight, youthful brows
- Aegyo-sal (under-eye "cute fat")
- Minimal eye makeup
- Focus on skin perfection
- Cute, youthful aesthetic

**Key Trends:**
- Glass skin
- Water glow
- Honey skin
- Cloudless skin
- Gradient lips

**Icons/Reference:** Korean idols, K-drama stars

### J-Beauty (Japanese)
**Philosophy:** Minimalism, quality, tradition, long-term skin health

**Characteristics:**
- Refined, sophisticated approach
- High-quality ingredients
- Scientific research-based
- Subtle, natural makeup
- Focus on skin texture
- Mochi skin (soft, pillowy)
- Less-is-more aesthetic
- Traditional meets modern
- Authenticity and reliability

**Key Trends:**
- Mochi skin
- Minimalist routines
- Quality over quantity

### C-Beauty (Chinese)
**Philosophy:** Atmospheric beauty, storytelling, cultural heritage

**Characteristics:**
- Bold, theatrical makeup
- "Atmospheric beauty" (mood/vibe creation)
- Drawing from Chinese opera and history
- Dramatic eye makeup (Western-influenced)
- Manhua lashes (comic-inspired)
- Rosy blush
- Gradient lips
- Glittery eyes
- Artistic, culturally rich

**Key Trends:**
- Douyin makeup
- Guochao (cultural pride in Chinese brands)
- Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) ingredients

**Modern Influence:** Less about "no-makeup makeup," more about transformation

### Middle Eastern Beauty
**Philosophy:** Eyes as windows to soul, dramatic enhancement, protection

**Characteristics:**
- Heavy eye emphasis (kohl)
- Dramatic, defined eyes
- Kohl liner (traditional stibnite/galena)
- Belief in evil eye protection
- Henna application
- Rich, bold colors
- Argan oil and rose water traditions
- Hammam rituals

**Key Elements:**
- Smokey, sultry eyes
- Full brows
- Bronze, warm skin tones
- Cultural and religious significance

### Indian Beauty
**Philosophy:** Cultural tradition, vibrant color, ceremonial significance

**Characteristics:**
- Rich, vibrant colors
- Intricate detailing
- Kumkum (red powder on forehead)
- Sindoor (married women's hairline mark)
- Bindi (third eye representation)
- Bold eyeshadows (especially weddings)
- Thick eyeliner
- Dramatic lashes
- Bright red or deep maroon lips
- Heavy jewelry coordination

**Occasions:** Weddings feature most elaborate makeup

### African Beauty
**Philosophy:** Natural hair celebration, indigenous ingredients, identity

**Characteristics:**
- Emphasis on natural hair care
- Shea butter and argan oil traditions
- Body art with symbolic meaning
- Henna and indigo patterns
- Makeup as identity marker
- Status and community representation
- Vibrant colors
- Celebrates diverse skin tones

**Regional Variations:**
- West African: Fulani henna patterns, indigo body art
- East African: Emphasis on skin care, natural beauty
- North African: Mediterranean influences

## 10. Editorial & Runway Classifications

### Fashion Editorial Makeup
**Purpose:** Complement clothing, tell story through fashion lens

**Characteristics:**
- Bold and experimental
- Supports fashion narrative
- May be less wearable
- Complements designer vision
- Runway-ready
- Often avant-garde

**Common Styles:**
- High fashion glam
- Deconstructed beauty
- Minimalist clean
- Bold color statements

### Beauty Editorial Makeup
**Purpose:** Showcase makeup and beauty products

**Characteristics:**
- Focus on makeup itself
- Product-highlighting
- Can range from natural to dramatic
- Skin is often the canvas
- Close-up friendly
- Detail-oriented

**Common Styles:**
- Skin-focused (dewy, matte, glass)
- Technique-focused (cut crease, strobing)
- Trend-focused (current viral looks)

### Runway/Fashion Week Makeup Trends

#### Spring/Summer 2024
- Shimmer and shine (metallics, chrome, glitter)
- Dewy, glowing skin
- Colorful eyeliner
- Graphic liner
- Floating eyeliner
- Fresh faces

#### Fall/Winter 2024
- Bold red lips
- Smokey eyes (rockstar girlfriend, emo-grunge)
- Grunge makeup (dark, earthy, lived-in)
- Minimalist clean beauty
- Natural, fluffy brows
- High-wattage blush

#### Paris Fashion Week Highlights
- Crystal appliqués as facial piercings
- Slicked-back wavy side bangs
- Black lips (Valentino)
- Bright pink inner corner shadow (Dior)
- Bold pops of color

### Editorial Categories

**High Fashion:**
- Avant-garde
- Unconventional
- Artistic expression
- May use non-makeup materials

**Commercial Editorial:**
- More wearable
- Product-focused
- Aspirational but achievable
- Trend-forward

**Conceptual:**
- Theme-driven
- Story-telling

- Artistic merit prioritized

**Beauty Close-Up:**
- Perfect skin

## 11. AI Prompting Guide for Beauty Looks

### Essential Prompt Components

#### 1. Main Subject Description
```
Example: "Portrait of a woman with [age/ethnicity] features"
```

#### 2. Makeup Style Category
Choose primary style:
- Natural makeup
- Soft glam
- Full glam
- Editorial makeup
- Avant-garde
- Bridal
- Fantasy/sci-fi

#### 3. Specific Makeup Elements

**Eyes:**
- Eye look: smokey, cat eye, cut crease, halo eye, natural
- Eyeshadow colors: neutral, bold, metallic, matte
- Eyeliner: winged, graphic, tightline, none
- Lashes: natural, dramatic, false lashes, volume

**Skin:**
- Finish: dewy, matte, satin, luminous, glossy
- Coverage: light, medium, full
- Features: freckles, glass skin, airbrushed, porcelain

**Lips:**
- Color: nude, red, pink, berry, dark
- Finish: matte, glossy, satin, velvet

**Cheeks:**
- Blush placement: apple, draping, sunburnt
- Contour: soft, dramatic, none
- Highlighter: strobing, subtle, wet look

#### 4. Era/Style Reference
```
Examples:
- "1940s Old Hollywood glamour"
- "Y2K frosted makeup"
- "90s grunge aesthetic"
- "Current clean girl aesthetic"
```

#### 5. Cultural Aesthetic (if applicable)
```
Examples:
- "K-beauty glass skin"
- "Middle Eastern dramatic eyes"
- "Indian bridal makeup"
```

#### 6. Technical Quality Terms
```
Essential for high-quality outputs:
- "High resolution, sharp details"
- "Studio photography with softbox lighting"
- "4K, ultra HD"
- "Hyperrealistic"
- "Cinematic"
- "Professional photography"
```

### Troubleshooting AI Beauty Prompts

**If results are too subtle:**
- Add "dramatic," "bold," "intense"
- Specify "highly pigmented," "saturated color"
- Include "editorial" or "magazine-quality"

**If results are too heavy:**
- Add "soft," "natural," "subtle"
- Specify "barely-there," "light touch"
- Include "wearable" or "everyday"

**If skin looks artificial:**
- Add "natural skin texture," "visible pores"
- Specify "realistic skin," "human skin detail"
- Include "not airbrushed" in negative prompt

**If colors are off:**
- Be very specific about shades: "warm terracotta" vs. "cool mauve"
- Reference real products or brands if helpful
- Specify undertones: "blue-red" vs. "orange-red"

## Professional Makeup Artist Terminology

**Blend:** Merging colors/products seamlessly without harsh lines
**Baking:** Setting makeup with heavy powder for extended time
**Buffing:** Circular motion application for smooth finish
**Stippling:** Dabbing motion for natural, airbrushed effect
**Feathering:** Softening edges, blending outward
**Patting:** Pressing product into skin rather than sweeping
**Building:** Layering product gradually for intensity
**Setting:** Using powder/spray to lock makeup in place
**Priming:** Prepping skin for makeup application
**Color Correcting:** Using opposite colors to neutralize discoloration
**Face Charting:** Sketching makeup plans on paper face templates
**Flashback:** White appearance in photos from certain ingredients
**Creasing:** Makeup settling into fine lines/wrinkles
**Fallout:** Eyeshadow particles falling onto cheeks
**Muddying:** Colors mixing and losing clarity
**Patchiness:** Uneven application with visible gaps
