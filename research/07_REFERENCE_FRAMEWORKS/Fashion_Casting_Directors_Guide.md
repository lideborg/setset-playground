## Introduction to Fashion Casting

Fashion casting is a specialized skill that combines aesthetic judgment, brand understanding, and the ability to identify models who can embody and communicate a designer's vision. Professional casting directors serve as the bridge between designers, brands, and modeling talent.

### Role of the Casting Director

- Represent the client to find the best talent for projects
- Review agency submissions and create shortlists
- Conduct in-person castings and evaluate model performance
- Match models to brand aesthetics and campaign objectives
- Manage option systems and booking confirmations
- Ensure diversity and inclusion in casting selections

### Key Responsibilities

1. **Pre-Production**: Meeting with designers weeks before shows to discuss references and direction
2. **Agency Coordination**: Reviewing packages and compiling lists of desired models
3. **Casting Sessions**: Meeting with every model from compiled lists
4. **Selection Process**: Narrowing from 40-60 choices to final 20-30 selections
5. **Option Management**: Handling first and second options on model availability
6. **Final Coordination**: Managing fittings and show-day logistics

## Casting Brief Structure and Components

A professional casting brief is a comprehensive document that outlines all requirements for a modeling project. It ensures clear communication between clients, agencies, and models.

### Essential Components

#### 1. Project Overview
- **Client/Brand Name**: Who is hiring
- **Project Type**: Editorial shoot, runway show, commercial campaign, lookbook
- **Creative Direction**: Overall concept and vision
- **Reference Images**: Mood boards or previous work examples
- **Usage Rights**: Where images will be used (print, digital, worldwide, regional)

#### 2. Physical Requirements

**Height Specifications**
- Female models: Specify range (e.g., 5'9" - 6'0" for runway, 5'6"+ for commercial)
- Male models: Specify range (e.g., 6'0" - 6'3" for runway)

**Measurements**
- Bust/Chest size
- Waist size
- Hip size
- Dress/Suit size (e.g., US 0-4, UK 8, EU 34-36)
- Shoe size

**Physical Characteristics**
- Age range (or age appearance)
- Ethnicity/diversity requirements
- Hair color, length, and style
- Eye color (if relevant)
- Skin tone
- Body type preferences

#### 3. Experience and Skills
- Portfolio requirements (e.g., editorial tearsheets, runway experience)
- Specific skills needed (e.g., runway walk, posing ability, acting)
- Previous campaign experience
- Social media following (if relevant for influencer campaigns)

#### 4. Personality and Presence
- Energy level (high-energy, calm, mysterious)
- Attitude alignment with brand
- Professional demeanor expectations
- Direction-taking ability

#### 5. Practical Information
- **Dates and Times**: Casting date, shoot/show dates, call times
- **Location**: Casting venue, shoot location
- **Duration**: Length of casting, project timeline
- **Rate/Compensation**: Budget range, usage fees
- **Availability Requirements**: Specific dates model must be available
- **What to Bring**: Portfolio, comp card, specific clothing items

#### 6. Look and Type Specifications
- Overall aesthetic (classic, edgy, commercial, high-fashion)
- Mood/vibe (approachable, mysterious, powerful, playful)
- Character or role model will portray
- Versatility needs

### Sample Casting Brief Template

```
PROJECT: [Brand Name] Spring/Summer 2025 Campaign
TYPE: Editorial Photoshoot
USAGE: Print and Digital, Worldwide

CREATIVE DIRECTION:
Modern minimalism with architectural elements. Clean, sophisticated aesthetic
emphasizing natural beauty and strong bone structure.

REQUIREMENTS:
- Female models, age 18-25 (or appear)
- Height: 5'9" - 5'11"
- Size: US 0-4 / UK 6-8 / EU 34-36
- Strong, defined facial features
- Natural, minimal makeup look
- All ethnicities encouraged

LOOK/TYPE:
- Editorial/High-Fashion
- Strong presence
- Versatile expressions
- Ability to embody both strength and grace

EXPERIENCE:
- Professional portfolio required
- Editorial experience preferred
- Comfortable with minimal direction

DATES:
- Casting: January 15, 2025 (10am-4pm)
- Shoot: January 22-24, 2025
- Location: New York City

TO BRING:
- Comp card
- Portfolio (digital acceptable)
- Wear: Black fitted tank, dark jeans, minimal makeup, hair natural
```

## Model Categories and Classifications

Understanding model categories is essential for effective casting. Each category has distinct characteristics, requirements, and market demands.

### Primary Categories

#### 1. Editorial/High-Fashion Models

**Definition**: Models featured in magazine editorials and high-end fashion publications (Vogue, Harper's Bazaar, Elle).

**Characteristics**:
- Unique, distinctive facial features
- Strong bone structure
- Photogenic from multiple angles
- Ability to convey emotion and tell stories through images
- Height: Typically 5'9"+ for women, 6'0"+ for men
- Size: US 0-4 for women, 40-42 jacket for men
- Age appearance: Usually 16-25

**Skills Required**:
- Exceptional posing ability
- Facial expression range
- Comfort with artistic/conceptual direction
- Minimal direction interpretation

**Market Focus**:
- Magazine editorials
- High-fashion campaigns
- Art-focused photography
- Designer collaborations

**Compensation Note**: Often lower day rates but high prestige value

#### 2. Runway/Catwalk Models

**Definition**: Models who walk in fashion shows and presentations.

**Characteristics**:
- Tall, lean physique
- Sample size fit (critical requirement)
- Strong runway walk and presence
- Height: 5'9" - 6'0" for women, 6'0" - 6'3" for men
- Measurements: 34-24-34 (women), 40-42 jacket, 32" waist (men)
- Ability to wear and showcase clothing effectively

**Skills Required**:
- Professional runway walk
- Quick changes backstage
- Ability to embody designer's vision
- Grace under pressure
- Stamina for long shows and fittings

**Market Focus**:
- Fashion Week shows (New York, Paris, Milan, London)
- Designer presentations
- Trunk shows
- Fashion events

**Key Requirement**: Must fit sample sizes (typically 34" bust, 24" waist, 34" hips for women)

#### 3. Commercial Models

**Definition**: Models for mainstream advertising, catalogs, and product promotion.

**Characteristics**:
- Relatable, approachable appearance
- Represents average consumer
- Diverse in age, size, height, and ethnicity
- Height: More flexible, often 5'6"+ for women
- Size: More flexible, US 2-8 common
- Age range: All ages (from children to seniors)

**Skills Required**:
- Acting ability (often required to deliver lines)
- Expressiveness and warmth
- Relatability and connection with camera
- Versatility across different scenarios
- Natural, genuine expressions

**Market Focus**:
- Brand campaigns
- Product advertising
- Catalogs
- E-commerce
- Billboard advertising
- Television commercials

**Compensation Note**: Typically higher rates than editorial due to usage and product sales correlation

#### 4. Lifestyle Models

**Definition**: Models portraying everyday scenarios and authentic moments.

**Characteristics**:
- Natural, candid appearance
- Relatable features
- Comfortable in casual settings
- All body types, ages, and ethnicities
- Authentic, non-posed aesthetic

**Skills Required**:
- Natural movement and interaction
- Comfort with improvisation
- Ability to portray genuine emotions
- Connection with other models (for group shoots)

**Market Focus**:
- Lifestyle brands
- Outdoor/adventure brands
- Family-focused campaigns
- Health and wellness
- Travel and hospitality

#### 5. Fit Models

**Definition**: Models used for garment fitting and sizing during design process.

**Characteristics**:
- Consistent, standard measurements
- Typically US size 8-10 (differs from runway)
- Proportionate body
- Reliable availability
- Professional understanding of garment construction

**Skills Required**:
- Patience during fittings
- Ability to provide feedback on fit and comfort
- Understanding of garment terminology
- Consistency in maintaining measurements

**Market Focus**:
- Design houses
- Apparel manufacturers
- Pattern making
- Sample production

#### 6. Plus-Size Models

**Definition**: Models typically size 8+ (US) representing diverse body types.

**Characteristics**:
- Size: US 8-18+ / UK 12-22+
- Height: 5'8"+ for runway, more flexible for commercial
- Proportionate measurements
- Confidence and body positivity

**Skills Required**:
- All standard modeling skills
- Strong presence and confidence
- Versatility in styling

**Market Focus**:
- Plus-size fashion brands
- Inclusive campaigns
- Mainstream brands embracing diversity
- Body-positive editorials

#### 7. Parts Models

**Definition**: Models specializing in specific body parts.

**Types**:
- Hand models (jewelry, watches, nail products)
- Foot models (shoes, nail polish)
- Leg models (hosiery, skincare)
- Hair models (hair products, salons)

**Characteristics**:
- Exceptional quality of specific body part
- Well-maintained features
- Consistent grooming

**Market Focus**:
- Product-specific advertising
- Close-up photography
- Detail shots

## Physical Attribute Evaluation Criteria

Professional casting directors assess models using a systematic framework that evaluates both aesthetic and practical considerations.

### Facial Features Assessment

#### Bone Structure
- **Definition**: Underlying skeletal framework that defines facial shape
- **Evaluation Points**:
  - Cheekbone prominence and definition
  - Jawline strength and definition
  - Chin proportion and shape
  - Forehead height and width
  - Symmetry and balance

**Descriptors**:
- Strong/Defined: Prominent, angular features
- Soft/Delicate: Rounded, gentle features
- Structured: Clear, architectural quality
- Sculpted: Well-defined, artistic quality
- Classic: Balanced, harmonious proportions

#### Eyes
- Position and spacing
- Size and shape
- Expression capability
- Color (when relevant to brief)
- Photogenic quality

**Descriptors**:
- Striking, captivating, expressive, intense, soft, wide-set, almond-shaped, doe-eyed

#### Facial Proportions
- Overall symmetry
- Feature balance
- Nose-to-face ratio
- Lip fullness and shape
- Face shape (oval, square, heart, round)

**Evaluation Categories**:
- **Classic Beauty**: Balanced, harmonious, universally appealing
- **Unique/Distinctive**: Memorable, unconventional, standout features
- **Character Face**: Interesting, story-telling quality
- **Commercial Appeal**: Relatable, friendly, approachable

### Body Assessment

#### Height and Proportions
- Overall height measurement
- Leg-to-torso ratio
- Neck length
- Arm length
- Overall balance

**Standards by Category**:
- Editorial/Runway Female: 5'9" - 6'0"
- Editorial/Runway Male: 6'0" - 6'3"
- Commercial Female: 5'6"+
- Commercial Male: 5'10"+

#### Body Measurements

**Female Standard Measurements** (Editorial/Runway):
- Bust: 32" - 34"
- Waist: 23" - 25"
- Hips: 33" - 35"
- Dress Size: US 0-4 / UK 6-8 / EU 34-36

**Male Standard Measurements** (Editorial/Runway):
- Chest: 38" - 40"
- Waist: 30" - 32"
- Suit Size: 38-40R
- Inseam: 32" - 34"

**Note**: Industry is increasingly embracing diverse sizing, but sample size requirements remain for runway work.

#### Physique Evaluation

**Descriptors**:
- **Lean**: Minimal body fat, defined muscle tone
- **Athletic**: Toned, strong appearance
- **Slender**: Naturally thin, delicate frame
- **Fit**: Healthy, toned, energetic appearance
- **Curvy**: Defined waist, proportionate curves
- **Statuesque**: Tall, commanding presence
- **Willowy**: Long, graceful limbs
- **Petite**: Compact, proportionate smaller frame

### Skin Evaluation

#### Skin Quality
- Clarity and evenness
- Texture
- Tone consistency
- Natural glow
- Photogenic quality

#### Skin Tone
- Undertone (warm, cool, neutral)
- Overall coloring
- Range flexibility for different lighting

**Professional Note**: All skin tones are valued; specific requirements depend on brand needs and diversity goals.

### Hair Assessment

#### Hair Characteristics
- Texture (straight, wavy, curly, coily)
- Thickness and volume
- Length
- Color
- Condition and health
- Versatility for styling

**Descriptors**:
- Lush, thick, voluminous, sleek, textured, natural, versatile, healthy, glossy

### Hands and Feet

#### Evaluation for Parts Work
- Shape and proportion
- Skin quality
- Nail condition
- Joint appearance
- Vein prominence (for hands)
- Arch definition (for feet)

### Movement and Posture

#### Physical Carriage
- Natural posture
- Grace of movement
- Body awareness
- Coordination
- Energy in motion

**Descriptors**:
- Graceful, fluid, confident, natural, poised, dynamic, languid, energetic

### Photogenic Quality

#### How Features Translate to Camera
- Angle versatility
- Lighting receptiveness
- Expression clarity
- Overall camera presence
- Consistency across shots

**Assessment Points**:
- Does the model photograph better than they appear in person?
- Do features remain strong from multiple angles?
- How do they respond to different lighting?
- Is there natural camera connection?

## Personality and Presence Assessment

Beyond physical attributes, professional casting directors evaluate intangible qualities that determine a model's success and fit for projects.

### Presence Evaluation

#### On-Camera Presence
**Definition**: The ability to command attention and connect through the lens

**Evaluation Criteria**:
- **Eye Contact**: Natural, engaging connection with camera
- **Comfort Level**: Ease in front of lens, lack of self-consciousness
- **Energy Projection**: Ability to convey emotion and mood
- **Consistency**: Maintaining presence across multiple shots
- **Authenticity**: Genuine expression vs. forced performance

**Descriptors**:
- Magnetic, captivating, natural, engaging, powerful, subtle, commanding, effortless

#### Runway Presence
**Definition**: The ability to own the catwalk and showcase garments effectively

**Evaluation Criteria**:
- **Walk Quality**: Confidence, rhythm, grace
- **Posture**: Strong, elongated, professional
- **Facial Expression**: Appropriate to collection mood
- **Energy**: Matches designer's vision
- **Garment Presentation**: Allows clothing to shine

**Descriptors**:
- Confident, powerful, graceful, commanding, fluid, dynamic, strong, poised

### Confidence Assessment

#### Indicators of Confidence
- Body language and posture
- Eye contact maintenance
- Responsiveness to direction
- Comfort with own appearance
- Ability to take risks with poses/expressions

#### Impact on Casting
- Confident models inspire trust from clients
- Projects assurance in final imagery
- Allows for minimal direction needs
- Indicates professionalism and experience

**Note**: Casting directors can immediately identify lack of confidence, which often disqualifies models regardless of physical attributes.

### Professional Demeanor

#### Professionalism Markers
- **Punctuality**: Arriving on time, prepared
- **Preparedness**: Bringing required materials, proper attire
- **Attitude**: Positive, cooperative, gracious
- **Communication**: Clear, respectful, professional
- **Adaptability**: Handling changes and challenges gracefully

#### Red Flags
- Lateness without communication
- Unprepared portfolio or appearance
- Negative attitude or complaints
- Difficulty taking direction
- Inappropriate behavior

### Personality Traits

#### Desirable Characteristics

**Versatility in Character**:
- Ability to embody different personas
- Range of emotional expression
- Adaptability to various concepts
- Chameleon-like transformation ability

**Work Ethic**:
- Dedication to craft
- Willingness to collaborate
- Positive attitude during long days
- Professional behavior under pressure

**Emotional Intelligence**:
- Reads room and adjusts accordingly
- Responds well to feedback
- Maintains composure
- Works well with team

**Curiosity and Engagement**:
- Interest in the creative process
- Questions that show involvement
- Enthusiasm for the project
- Desire to understand vision

### Direction-Taking Ability

#### Evaluation Points
- **Speed of Interpretation**: How quickly model understands direction
- **Execution Accuracy**: How precisely model delivers requested pose/expression
- **Creative Input**: Ability to add own interpretation while following direction
- **Adjustment Capability**: Making immediate changes when asked

#### Direction Response Categories

**Excellent Direction-Taker**:
- Interprets with minimal instruction
- Anticipates photographer's needs
- Offers variations on direction
- Maintains consistency

**Average Direction-Taker**:
- Requires clear, specific instruction
- Follows direction accurately
- Limited interpretation
- Needs repetition occasionally

**Needs Development**:
- Requires extensive explanation
- Struggles with abstract concepts
- Inconsistent execution
- Needs frequent correction

### Versatility in Expression

#### Emotional Range
Ability to convey different moods and emotions:
- **Joy/Happiness**: Genuine smile, lightness, warmth
- **Confidence**: Power, strength, assurance
- **Mystery**: Intrigue, aloofness, enigma
- **Sensuality**: Allure without overtness
- **Vulnerability**: Softness, openness, delicacy
- **Intensity**: Focused, serious, dramatic
- **Playfulness**: Fun, lighthearted, energetic

#### Expression Quality Assessment
- **Natural vs. Forced**: Authenticity of emotion
- **Subtlety**: Ability to convey without over-acting
- **Range**: Diversity of expressions in portfolio
- **Control**: Ability to access emotions on command

### Energy Management

#### Energy Type Matching
Different projects require different energy levels:

**High Energy Projects**:
- Activewear campaigns
- Youth-focused brands
- Dynamic editorials
- Fast fashion

**Moderate Energy Projects**:
- Contemporary brands
- Lifestyle campaigns
- Catalog work
- E-commerce

**Low Energy Projects**:
- Luxury brands
- Fine jewelry
- Artistic editorials
- Minimal aesthetics

#### Adaptability in Energy
- Can model shift between energy levels?
- Does natural energy match project needs?
- Is energy authentic or performed?

### Interpersonal Skills

#### Team Collaboration
- Works well with other models
- Respectful to crew and staff
- Positive on-set presence
- Professional boundaries

#### Client Interaction
- Appropriate communication with clients
- Understanding of business relationship
- Professionalism in meetings
- Social media conduct

## Versatility Evaluation Framework

Versatility is one of the most valued attributes in modeling, as it increases a model's bookability and career longevity.

### Components of Versatility

#### 1. Visual Transformation Range

**Hair Versatility**:
- Can style be changed dramatically?
- Does model look good with various hairstyles?
- Is hair healthy enough for frequent styling?
- Range: straight, wavy, curly, updos, natural

**Makeup Transformation**:
- Natural/minimal makeup appearance
- Dramatic/editorial makeup capability
- Character transformation with makeup
- Range: fresh-faced to avant-garde

**Styling Adaptability**:
- High-fashion editorial looks
- Commercial/relatable styling
- Vintage/period styling
- Contemporary/street style
- Luxury/sophisticated styling

#### 2. Expression and Mood Range

**Evaluation Through Portfolio Review**:
- Count distinct moods/expressions shown
- Assess range from subtle to dramatic
- Evaluate transformation between shots
- Check for repeated or limited expressions

**Range Categories**:
- **Limited Range** (1-3 distinct looks): Needs development
- **Moderate Range** (4-6 distinct looks): Professional baseline
- **Excellent Range** (7+ distinct looks): High versatility

**Mood Spectrum**:
- Serious ↔ Playful
- Powerful ↔ Vulnerable
- Mysterious ↔ Open
- Edgy ↔ Classic
- Intense ↔ Relaxed

#### 3. Genre Versatility

**Assessment Questions**:
- Can model work across multiple modeling categories?
- Portfolio shows range of work types?
- Adaptable to different industry sectors?

**Genre Categories**:
- Editorial/High-Fashion
- Commercial/Catalog
- Runway
- Lifestyle
- Beauty
- Fitness/Athletic

**Scoring**:
- **Specialist** (1-2 genres): Strong in niche
- **Multi-Genre** (3-4 genres): Professional versatility
- **Universal** (5+ genres): Exceptional range

#### 4. Brand Aesthetic Range

**Can Model Represent Different Brand Types?**:
- Luxury/High-End (Chanel, Dior, Hermès)
- Contemporary (Zara, & Other Stories, Everlane)
- Fast Fashion (H&M, Forever 21, ASOS)
- Outdoor/Athletic (Patagonia, Nike, Lululemon)
- Bohemian/Alternative (Free People, Urban Outfitters)
- Classic/Traditional (Ralph Lauren, Brooks Brothers)
- Avant-Garde (Comme des Garçons, Rick Owens)

#### 5. Pose and Movement Versatility

**Static Posing Range**:
- Strong, powerful poses
- Soft, flowing poses
- Dynamic, energetic poses
- Minimal, subtle poses
- Editorial/artistic poses
- Natural, candid poses

**Movement Capability**:
- Runway walk variations
- Action/dynamic movement
- Dance-influenced movement
- Natural, lifestyle movement
- Controlled, deliberate movement

### Portfolio Versatility Assessment

#### Scoring System

**Points Allocation** (out of 100):

1. **Visual Range** (25 points):
   - Hair versatility (5 points)
   - Makeup range (5 points)
   - Styling diversity (10 points)
   - Overall transformation (5 points)

2. **Expression Range** (25 points):
   - Mood diversity (10 points)
   - Emotional depth (10 points)
   - Subtlety vs. drama (5 points)

3. **Genre Diversity** (20 points):
   - Number of modeling types shown (10 points)
   - Quality within each type (10 points)

4. **Brand Aesthetic Range** (15 points):
   - Luxury capability (5 points)
   - Commercial appeal (5 points)
   - Contemporary edge (5 points)

5. **Technical Skill** (15 points):
   - Posing variety (5 points)
   - Angle work (5 points)
   - Consistency (5 points)

**Rating Scale**:
- 85-100: Exceptional Versatility
- 70-84: High Versatility
- 55-69: Moderate Versatility
- 40-54: Limited Versatility
- Below 40: Specialist/Needs Development

### Development Recommendations

#### For Models with Limited Versatility

**Short-Term Actions**:
- Work with photographers on diverse test shoots
- Practice expression range in mirror
- Study editorial poses from magazines
- Experiment with different styling approaches

**Long-Term Development**:
- Build portfolio with intentional variety
- Take acting classes for expression work
- Work with different photographers/stylists
- Develop signature looks in multiple styles

#### For Models with Moderate Versatility

**Enhancement Strategies**:
- Identify weaker areas in range
- Add specific portfolio pieces to fill gaps
- Push boundaries in current comfort zones
- Collaborate on experimental projects

#### For Highly Versatile Models

**Maintenance and Growth**:
- Continue pushing creative boundaries
- Maintain diverse portfolio
- Avoid becoming typecast
- Seek challenging projects

### Versatility in Context

#### When High Versatility is Critical
- Agency roster building (want multi-market models)
- Long-term brand ambassadorships
- Campaign work requiring multiple looks
- Models seeking career longevity

#### When Specialization is Valuable
- Niche markets (petite, plus, mature)
- Signature look that books consistently
- Highly specific brand aesthetics
- Parts modeling

## Matching Models to Brand Aesthetics

Successful casting requires understanding brand DNA and finding models who naturally embody that aesthetic.

### Understanding Brand Aesthetics

#### Brand Analysis Framework

**Visual Identity**:
- Color palette preferences
- Photography style (minimal, dramatic, natural, editorial)
- Styling approach (polished, casual, experimental)
- Overall mood and tone

**Target Demographic**:
- Age range
- Lifestyle characteristics
- Economic positioning
- Values and aspirations

**Brand Personality**:
- Sophisticated vs. Approachable
- Classic vs. Contemporary
- Minimal vs. Maximal
- Serious vs. Playful

**Brand Values**:
- Sustainability/Ethics
- Diversity and inclusion
- Innovation vs. Tradition
- Luxury vs. Accessibility

### Brand Aesthetic Categories

#### 1. Luxury/Heritage Brands
**Examples**: Chanel, Hermès, Dior, Ralph Lauren

**Model Requirements**:
- Classic, refined features
- Sophisticated presence
- Elegant, graceful movement
- Timeless beauty
- Polished professionalism

**Look Descriptors**:
- Elegant, refined, sophisticated, timeless, polished, graceful, distinguished

**Avoid**:
- Overly trendy or contemporary looks
- Extreme styling or edgy aesthetics
- Heavy tattoos or unconventional modifications

#### 2. Contemporary/Modern Brands

## Editorial vs. Commercial Casting

Understanding the fundamental differences between editorial and commercial modeling is essential for effective casting.

### Editorial Casting

#### Purpose and Goals
- Tell a story or convey artistic concept
- Showcase fashion as art
- Build brand prestige and image
- Create aspirational content
- Demonstrate creative vision

#### Physical Requirements
**Strict Standards**:
- Height: 5'9" - 6'0" (women), 6'0" - 6'3" (men)
- Size: US 0-4 (women), 40-42 jacket (men)
- Specific measurements required
- Sample size fit critical
- Strong bone structure emphasized

#### Look and Type Preferences
- Unique, distinctive features
- Photogenic from multiple angles
- Transformative capability
- High-fashion aesthetic
- Artistic appeal

#### Skills Required
- **Posing Expertise**: Complex, artistic poses
- **Expression Range**: Subtle to dramatic
- **Minimal Direction**: Interpret abstract concepts
- **Still Photography**: Comfort with long shoots
- **Versatility**: Multiple looks/moods

#### Evaluation Priorities
1. **Visual Impact**: How compelling in images
2. **Uniqueness**: Distinctive, memorable features
3. **Versatility**: Range of transformation
4. **Editorial Portfolio**: Previous magazine work
5. **Artistic Interpretation**: Understanding concepts

#### Compensation Structure
- Lower day rates typically
- Usage often "editorial only" (lower fees)
- Value in prestige and portfolio building
- Unpaid or TFP (trade for print) for emerging models
- Higher rates for established editorial models

#### Market Focus
- Fashion magazines (Vogue, Harper's Bazaar, Elle)
- High-fashion campaigns
- Designer lookbooks
- Artistic photography projects
- Fashion week presentations

### Commercial Casting

#### Purpose and Goals
- Sell products or services
- Connect with target consumers
- Create relatable content
- Drive purchasing decisions
- Represent brand values

#### Physical Requirements
**Flexible Standards**:
- Height: More flexible, 5'6"+ common (women)
- Size: Diverse, US 2-12+ (women)
- Age: All ages, from children to seniors
- Body types: Varied, representing consumers
- Fewer strict measurement requirements

#### Look and Type Preferences
- Relatable, approachable appearance
- Friendly, warm features
- Represents target demographic
- Diverse representation valued
- Commercial appeal over editorial uniqueness

#### Skills Required
- **Acting Ability**: Often deliver lines or scenarios
- **Expressiveness**: Genuine emotion and warmth
- **Relatability**: Connect with average consumer
- **Versatility**: Multiple product categories
- **Movement**: Natural, lifestyle actions

#### Evaluation Priorities
1. **Relatability**: Target audience connection
2. **Authenticity**: Genuine, natural quality
3. **Versatility**: Range across products
4. **Acting Skills**: Ability to convey scenarios
5. **Commercial Portfolio**: Previous brand work

#### Compensation Structure
- Higher day rates than editorial
- Usage fees based on media and territory
- Buyout options for broad usage
- Residuals possible for TV commercials
- Compensation tied to product sales/exposure

#### Market Focus
- Brand campaigns and advertising
- Product catalogs
- E-commerce imagery
- Billboard and print advertising
- Television commercials
- Digital marketing

### Comparative Analysis

#### Key Differences

| Aspect | Editorial | Commercial |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| **Primary Goal** | Artistic expression | Product sales |
| **Audience** | Fashion industry, aspirational | General consumers |
| **Model Look** | Unique, high-fashion | Relatable, diverse |
| **Height Req.** | Strict (5'9"+) | Flexible (5'6"+) |
| **Size Req.** | Sample size (0-4) | Varied (2-12+) |
| **Age Range** | Typically 16-28 | All ages |
| **Skills Focus** | Posing, expression | Acting, relatability |
| **Compensation** | Lower rates, prestige | Higher rates, usage fees |
| **Usage Rights** | Editorial only | Broad, multiple media |
| **Direction Style** | Abstract, artistic | Specific, product-focused |
| **Booking Volume** | Less frequent | More frequent |

#### Casting Approach Differences

**Editorial Casting Process**:
1. Review agency packages for unique faces
2. Prioritize strong editorial portfolios
3. Assess artistic interpretation ability
4. Test with directional, conceptual brief
5. Consider transformation potential
6. Evaluate sample size fit (critical)
7. Select for story-telling capability

**Commercial Casting Process**:
1. Define target demographic clearly
2. Seek relatable, diverse representation
3. Assess acting and expression skills
4. Test with product interaction scenarios
5. Evaluate authenticity and warmth
6. Consider broad market appeal
7. Select for genuine connection

### Models Who Excel in Both

#### Crossover Success Factors
- **Versatility**: Can shift between aesthetics
- **Range**: Editorial edge + commercial warmth
- **Physical Attributes**: Meet both requirement sets
- **Skills**: Posing artistry + acting ability
- **Portfolio**: Demonstrates both capabilities

#### Building Dual-Market Portfolios
- **Editorial Sections**: Art-focused, high-fashion, editorial tears
- **Commercial Sections**: Product work, relatable imagery, brand campaigns
- **Versatility Demonstration**: Range between both styles
- **Strategic Development**: Balance prestige with bookability

## Fashion Week Casting Process

Fashion Week represents the pinnacle of runway casting, with established protocols and intense timelines.

### Timeline and Structure

#### Pre-Show Preparation (4-6 weeks before)

**Week 1-2: Initial Planning**
- Casting director meets with designer
- Discuss collection theme and references
- Review previous season's cast
- Establish casting direction and budget
- Create initial wish list of models

**Week 3-4: Agency Submissions**
- Agencies submit model packages
- Digital portfolios with comp cards
- Model availability confirmations
- Option requests begin
- Casting director creates shortlist

**Week 5-6: Pre-Casting Review**
- Review model reels and portfolios
- Compile casting lists (100-200 models)
- Schedule casting sessions
- Coordinate with multiple shows (avoiding conflicts)

#### Fashion Week Period (1 week)

**Days 1-3: Intense Casting**
- Back-to-back model appointments
- 5-minute slots per model
- Multiple castings per day (10-15 shows)
- Option management begins
- Narrowing selections

**Days 4-5: Selection and Confirmation**
- Final selections presented to designer
- First and second options placed
- Negotiations with agencies
- Confirmations secured
- Backup models identified

**Days 6-7: Fittings and Final Prep**
- Models try on show pieces
- Walking order determined
- Final adjustments made
- Show day logistics confirmed

### Casting Session Structure

#### Standard Casting Flow (5 minutes per model)

**Minute 1: Check-In**
- Model presents comp card
- Portfolio review (if needed)
- Measurements verified
- Professional pleasantries

**Minute 2-3: Runway Walk**
- Model walks in casting space
- Demonstrates walk quality
- May try different attitudes/energy
- Casting director observes movement

**Minute 4: Photos/Video**
- Digitals captured (front, side, back)
- Video of walk recorded
- Close-up shots of face
- Full-body captures

**Minute 5: Brief Conversation**
- Availability confirmation
- Professional demeanor assessment
- Any specific questions
- Thank you and dismissal

### What Casting Directors Look For

#### Immediate Assessment Points

**First Impression (Within 30 seconds)**:
- Confidence and presence
- Physical appearance and grooming
- Energy and professionalism
- Natural runway walk
- Overall "it" factor

**Quote from Industry**:
"When a model walks into the room, we look for presence and confidence—if she doesn't have it or isn't ready, we've already decided she's not right for that season."

#### Detailed Evaluation

**Physical Checklist**:
- Sample size fit (34-24-34 standard)
- Height in person vs. on paper
- Proportions and body symmetry
- Skin condition
- Hair quality and versatility
- Current fitness level

**Performance Evaluation**:
- **Walk Quality**: Rhythm, posture, confidence
- **Adaptability**: Can adjust walk style
- **Presence**: Commands attention naturally
- **Face**: Expressions while walking
- **Energy**: Matches collection mood

**Professional Assessment**:
- Punctuality and preparedness
- Attitude and demeanor
- Communication skills
- Responses to direction
- Overall professionalism

### Option System

#### Understanding Options

**First Option**:
- Model held exclusively for client
- Client has priority for booking
- Must be released if not confirmed
- Typically 24-48 hours to confirm
- Model cannot accept other bookings

**Second Option**:
- Another client has first priority
- Model available if first releases
- Backup position
- Less secure but still in consideration

**Confirmed/Booked**:
- Model officially secured for show
- Contract and rate agreed
- Fitting scheduled
- Exclusive to this show (typically)

#### Option Management Strategy

**For Casting Directors**:
- Place first options on top choices
- Have second options as backups
- Balance wish list with reality
- Negotiate with agencies
- Manage conflicts across multiple shows

**For Models/Agencies**:
- Balance multiple options
- Prioritize prestige vs. payment
- Manage schedule conflicts
- Build relationships with casting directors
- Strategic acceptance of bookings

### Selection Process

#### Narrowing Down (100+ to 20-30 models)

**Round 1: Initial Review** (100-200 models seen)
- Eliminate obvious mismatches
- Note strong contenders
- Consider sample size fit
- Create maybe list

**Round 2: Designer Review** (40-60 presented)
- Show designer selects/compiled images
- Discuss each model's fit
- Designer makes selections
- Casting director advises

**Round 3: Final Casting** (20-30 confirmed)
- Secure first options
- Negotiate rates
- Handle conflicts and releases
- Confirm final lineup

**Round 4: Fittings** (Final cast)
- Models try actual show pieces
- Walking order determined
- Last-minute adjustments
- Show preparation begins

## Industry Standard Terminology

A comprehensive glossary of professional fashion casting and modeling terminology.

### Casting-Specific Terms

**Casting/Casting Call**:
Notice for modeling job, detailing model requirements for upcoming production. Can be open (public) or closed (invitation only).

**Casting Brief**:
Document outlining requirements for models including physical characteristics, experience, skills, personality, and availability.

**Casting Director**:
Professional who represents client to find best talent for project. Coordinates between agencies, models, and clients.

**Go-See**:
Informal casting appointment where model meets with client or casting director. Less formal than official casting.

**Casting Tape/Reel**:
Video compilation of model's walk, poses, and presence, used for review by casting directors and clients.

**Digitals**:
Simple, unretouched photos of model (front, side, back, close-up face) showing current appearance without makeup or styling.

**Option/Hold**:
Temporary hold on model's availability for potential booking.

**First Option**:
Exclusive hold; client has priority to book model. Model cannot accept other bookings without client releasing option.

**Second Option**:
Backup hold; another client has first priority. Model available if first option is released.

**Confirmed/Booked**:
Model officially secured for job with agreed terms and signed contract.

**Direct Booking**:
When agency secures booking for model without go-see or casting (based on portfolio/previous work).

**Release**:
When client releases option hold on model, freeing them to accept other bookings.

### Model Categories

**Editorial Model**:
Model specializing in magazine editorials and high-fashion photography. Unique features, strong poses, artistic interpretation.

**Commercial Model**:
Model for mainstream advertising, catalogs, product promotion. Relatable appearance, diverse types, acting ability.

**Runway Model/Catwalk Model**:
Model specializing in fashion shows. Must fit sample size, have strong walk, typically 5'9"+ (women) or 6'0"+ (men).

**Fit Model**:
Model with standard measurements used for garment fitting during design process. Typically size 8-10 (US).

**Plus-Size Model**:
Model typically size 8+ (US), representing diverse body types in fashion and advertising.

**Lifestyle Model**:
Model portraying authentic, everyday scenarios. Natural, candid aesthetic for relatable brand imagery.

**High-Fashion Model**:
Model with strong editorial and runway presence. Distinctive features, sample size fit, artistic capability.

**Parts Model**:
Model specializing in specific body parts (hands, feet, legs, hair) for close-up product shots.

**New Faces**:
New models in development phase of careers. Building portfolios, gaining experience, emerging talent.

### Agency and Representation

**Mother Agency**:
Model's primary/home agency that discovered and develops them. Coordinates with other agencies globally.

**Agency Board**:
Roster of models represented by agency. Can be main board, development board, special/curve board, etc.

**Development Board**:
Agency roster of new models being developed before moving to main board.

**Comp Card/Composite Card/Zed Card**:
Printed card with model's best photos, measurements, and contact information. Essential marketing tool.

**Model Portfolio/Book**:
Collection of model's best work showing range and capabilities. Physical or digital.

**Tear Sheets**:
Published pages torn from magazines showing model's editorial work. Proves publication experience.

**Test Shoot/TFP (Trade for Print)**:
Collaborative photo shoot where model and photographer work without payment, trading time for portfolio images.

**Scouting**:
Process of discovering new modeling talent. Can be in-person, digital, or through competitions.

**Open Call**:
Scheduled time when agencies accept walk-in model applicants without appointments.

### Booking and Business

**Day Rate**:
Model's fee for one day of work (typically 8-10 hours).

**Half-Day Rate**:
Fee for shorter booking (typically 4-5 hours).

**Buyout**:
Payment for unlimited usage of images, typically higher than standard usage fees.

**Usage Rights/Usage Fees**:
Fees for how, where, and how long client can use model's images. Specified by media, territory, and duration.

**Exclusivity**:
Agreement preventing model from working for competing brands within specified category and timeframe.

**Editorial Usage**:
Images used only for magazine editorials, not advertising. Lower fees than commercial usage.

**Commercial Usage**:
Images used for advertising and product promotion. Higher fees than editorial.

**Worldwide Rights**:
Permission to use images globally, across all territories.

**Territory**:
Geographic region where images can be used (e.g., North America, Europe, Asia).

**Duration**:
Time period client has rights to use images (e.g., 6 months, 1 year, 2 years).

**Residuals**:
Additional payments to model when commercial airs or prints beyond original agreement.

### Physical and Styling Terms

**Sample Size**:
Standard size for designer samples, typically 34-24-34 (bust-waist-hips) or US 0-4 for women, 40R suit for men.

**Measurements**:
Model's bust/chest, waist, hips, height, and shoe size. Critical information for all bookings.

**Fitting**:
Appointment where model tries on clothing for client to assess fit and make adjustments.

**Pencil**:
Informal hold or tentative booking. Less secure than option.

**Stats**:
Model's vital statistics: height, measurements, shoe size, hair/eye color.

**Height Card**:
Card or notation verifying model's actual measured height (some models exaggerate).

### On-Set and Production

**Call Time**:
Time model must arrive on set or at location.

**Wrap**:
End of shoot or production day.

**On Hold**:
See "Option" - model's availability temporarily held for potential booking.

**Confirmed**:
Booking officially secured with signed agreement.

**Cancelled**:
Booking that was confirmed but then cancelled. May involve cancellation fees.

**Client**:
Person or company hiring model (brand, photographer, magazine, designer).

**Production**:
Overall shoot or project, including all crew, planning, and execution.

**Mood Board**:
Collection of reference images showing desired aesthetic, inspiration, and direction for shoot.

**Treatment**:
Document describing creative concept, vision, and execution plan for campaign or editorial.

### Runway-Specific Terms

**Runway/Catwalk**:
Raised platform where models walk to showcase clothing during fashion shows.

**Walking Order/Lineup**:
Order in which models appear in fashion show.

**Opening Look**:
First outfit/model to appear in fashion show. High-profile placement.

**Closing Look**:
Final outfit/model in fashion show, often wedding dress or statement piece. Prestigious placement.

**Finale Walk**:
Final walk where all models return to stage together, often with designer.

**Backstage**:
Behind-scenes area during fashion show where models prepare, change, and wait.

**Dresser**:
Person assigned to help model with quick changes during show.

**Quick Change**:
Rapid costume change between runway appearances, often under 60 seconds.

**Show Package**:
Materials sent to casting directors including model lineup, show concept, and booking details.

### Photography and Posing

**Editorial Pose**:
Artistic, fashion-forward pose suitable for magazine editorials. Often unconventional or dramatic.

**Commercial Pose**:
Natural, relatable pose suitable for product advertising. Approachable and clear.

**Three-Quarter Pose**:
Body angled approximately 45 degrees from camera, classic flattering angle.

**Profile Shot**:
Side view of model showcasing profile and bone structure.

**Candid**:
Natural, unposed shot capturing genuine moment or expression.

**Hero Shot**:
Main, featured image from shoot. Best representation of campaign or concept.

**Beauty Shot**:
Close-up focused on face, makeup, and beauty. Often for cosmetics or skincare.

**Full-Length/Full-Body**:
Shot showing model from head to toe.

### Industry Slang and Expressions

**"It" Factor**:
Intangible quality that makes model captivating and memorable. Natural star quality.

**"Booker"**:
Agent who manages model's bookings, schedule, and career.

**"On Board"**:
Signed with modeling agency.

**"Getting Picked Up"**:
Being signed by agency or booked for job.

**"Walking For"**:
Modeling in designer's runway show (e.g., "walking for Chanel").

**"Shot By"**:
Photographed by specific photographer (e.g., "shot by Steven Meisel").

**"Tear"**:
Short for "tear sheet" - published editorial page.

**"Book Out"**:
Informing agency of dates model is unavailable.

**"Availability"**:
Dates and times model is free to work.

**"Rate"**:
Model's fee or day rate for work.

### Descriptive Terms for Models

**Look**:
Overall aesthetic, style, and vibe model projects.

**Type**:
Category or niche model fits into (commercial, editorial, girl-next-door, edgy, etc.).

**Presence**:
Model's ability to command attention and engage viewer.

**Range**:
Model's versatility across different looks, moods, and styles.

**Energy**:
Vibe and intensity model brings (high-energy, calm, powerful, playful, etc.).

**Bone Structure**:
Underlying facial structure. "Strong bone structure" indicates defined, angular features.

**Photogenic**:
Quality of photographing well; looking better in photos than in person.

**Chameleon**:
Model who can dramatically transform appearance across different styling and concepts.

**Natural**:
Model with effortless, authentic quality; looks comfortable and genuine.

**Versatile**:
Model capable of working across multiple categories, styles, and markets.

### Diversity and Inclusion Terms

**Inclusive Casting**:
Casting that represents diverse ethnicities, body types, ages, genders, and abilities.

**Diverse Board**:
Agency roster representing variety of looks, ethnicities, sizes, and types.

**Curve Model**:
Alternative term for plus-size model, emphasizing body shape rather than size.

**Non-Binary Model**:
Model who identifies outside traditional gender binary.

**Petite Model**:
Model under 5'7" height, often for specific petite fashion markets.

**Mature Model**:
Model over 40 (or sometimes 30+), representing older demographic.

**All-Inclusive**:
Casting open to all types, sizes, ages, ethnicities without restrictions.

## Conclusion

Professional fashion casting is a sophisticated discipline requiring deep understanding of aesthetics, brand identity, market dynamics, and human potential. Successful casting directors balance artistic vision with commercial reality, identifying models who not only meet physical criteria but embody the intangible qualities that bring fashion to life.

### Key Takeaways

1. **Systematic Evaluation**: Professional casting uses structured frameworks to assess models across physical attributes, presence, versatility, and brand fit.

2. **Beyond Physical**: While measurements and proportions matter, personality, presence, and professionalism often determine casting decisions.

3. **Brand Alignment**: Matching models to brand aesthetics requires understanding brand DNA, target audience, and market positioning.

4. **Category Understanding**: Different modeling categories (editorial, commercial, runway, lifestyle) have distinct requirements and evaluation criteria.

5. **Industry Evolution**: Fashion casting is becoming more inclusive, diverse, and representative, while maintaining professional standards.

6. **Professional Standards**: Major agencies and casting directors maintain consistent standards while embracing unique beauty and individual style.

### Best Practices for Casting Directors

- Develop comprehensive casting briefs with clear criteria
- Use systematic evaluation frameworks for consistency
- Balance aesthetic vision with practical requirements
- Prioritize diversity and inclusive representation
- Build strong relationships with agencies and models
- Stay current with industry trends and standards
- Advocate for ethical treatment and fair practices
- Document decisions and maintain professional records

### The Future of Fashion Casting

The industry continues evolving toward greater inclusivity, authenticity, and diversity. Technology enables global scouting and virtual casting, while social media creates new pathways for discovery. Despite these changes, the fundamental principles remain: finding individuals who embody brand vision, connect with audiences, and bring fashion to compelling visual life.
